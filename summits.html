Olympian

Sarah Wells

Obstacles don’t scare Sarah Wells. As a 400m hurdler, this Olympian’s reputation was forged through overcoming challenges, redefining realistic and achieving the incredible. Take her debut at the London Olympics in 2012, which came despite an injury that had her sidelined her for months just the year before.

Leaning in to innovative training techniques and a modified training plan, Sarah fought to realize her dream. Evidently someone who understands the importance of managing pressure and the importance of mindset, you'll want to listen up while Olympic semi-finalist and Pan Am Games silver medalist, Sarah Wells takes the stage.

Environmentalist

Dillon Mendes

Dillon Mendes co-founded the social enterprise PickWaste, which has, and continues to have, a massive impact on the environment. Dillon is a true believer in the Power of One. After an encounter with a friend in high school, he pivoted his direction in life and realized he had the power to ignite change. He speaks about how one person, one action, or one movement can change the world. Dillon has already had the opportunity to share this message with over 9000 students and is not planning on stopping there

Dillon is a Top 25 Under 25 Canadian Environmental Changemaker award winner, and a Top 35 Under 35 Global Changemaker winner. Dillon is a social entrepreneur, TEDx & youth speaker as well as a student at the University of Waterloo.

Born This Way

Shadille Estepan

Shadille Estepan is Communications and Outreach Manager for Lady Gaga's Born This Way Foundation.  Shadille supports the Foundation’s external communications efforts – including video production – as well as the organization’s programming. Shadille has been part of Born This Way Foundation since 2012 when she served as part of the inaugural Youth Advisory Board. Shadille is originally from Miami and she is a graduate of the University of Florida.

Peace Advocates

James and George Tucker

James and George Tucker, two brothers from rural Sierra Leone, have an extraordinary story. As young boys in the Blood Diamond War in 1990’s, the leadership and courage they showed during this time would change the course of their lives.  James and George came out of the Diamond War as peace and gender equity activists, working hard getting girls in to school, lowering levels of violence in classrooms, and training teachers on peaceful classroom management skills.

When Ebola struck their already struggling West-African country in 2014, James and George took crucial steps that would save their village from the outbreak. James and George are leaving rural Sierra Leone for the first time to come to Vision2020

Day TWO
Expansion Workshops

Personal Leadership in Sports

  • For Olympian Sarah Wells, the podium has always been the destination however; it was the challenging moments in-between that made Sarah the champion she is today.

    Sarah will discuss the importance of believing in your goals, share different types of training, proper nutrition, and the mindset she used to be successful in sports.

Climate Change

  • A lot of environmentalists talk about the global issues that this world currently faces but fail to motivate individuals to start taking action. Dillon empowers individuals to think globally but act locally to make a change in their society.

    This will be a practical session that and you will leave with immediate actions you can do to help with climate change and protecting the environment.

RBC Your Brand Matters

  • The Your Brand Matters Workshop is a workshop to help young people build awareness of their brand, and learn how to use their brand effectively throughout their career. Young people will learn the essentials of what makes a great personal brand, first impression, professional presence, and elevator pitch.

Positive Mental Health

  • The total number of 12-19 year olds in Canada at risk for developing depression is a staggering 3.2 million. Students reporting feelings of anxiety since 2012 are rising at alarming rates. This session is specially designed for students and teachers who would like to learn more about anxiety, depression and self-harm. Participants will leave with a greater understanding of how to help maintain positive mental health, how to recognize symptoms and how to implement strategies to help those who are struggling.
